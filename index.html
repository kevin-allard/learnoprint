Does the correlation imply causation? Explain why or why not.

The number of shark attacks on a certain beach increases on days when more ice cream is purchased.

  1. Yes, the correlation implies causation. The increase in ice cream sales increases the number of sharks present in the area, so the likelihood of a shark attack increases.
  2. Yes, the correlation implies causation. The increase in ice cream sales increases the number of visitors who enter the water. This increases the opportunity for shark attacks.
  3. No, the correlation does not imply causation. There are likely more people present at the beach when sales are high, so the increase in the number of people increases the opportunity for shark attacks.
  4. No, the correlation does not imply causation. There are likely fewer people present at the beach when sales are high, so the decrease in the number of people increases the likelihood of a shark attack.

Correct Answer: C
